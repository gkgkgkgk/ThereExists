# Probes
Probes are unmanned vehicles that can be sent to explore distant star systems. They are equipped with a variety of sensors and can be used to collect data about the environment, broadcast signals, or perform other tasks. Probes can be launched from a ship or from a planet. 

## Probe Types
There are many different types of probes, each with its own strengths and weaknesses. Below are some examples of probe types:

* **Satellites** - Satellites are used to collect data about the environment from orbit.
* **Landers** - Landers are used to collect data about the environment from the surface of a planet or moon, and are typically stationary once landed.
* **Rovers** - Rovers are used to collect data about the environment from the surface of a planet or moon, and can be used to explore large areas.
* **Sample Return Probes** - Sample return probes are used to collect samples of a planet or moon and return them to the spacecraft.
* **Atmospheric Scoops** - Atmospheric scoops are used to collect samples of a planet's atmosphere.
* **Tethered Probes** - Tethered probes are probes that are connected to a spacecraft by a tether.

The sensors, power requirements, size, and other attributes of each probe type will vary depending on the specific probe. For example, you may have a satellite probe that takes pictures of planets from orbit and is equipped with a powerful telescope, while a lander might be equipped with a drill to gather and analyze samples from below the surface. 

## Probe Usage
Once a probe is launched from your spacecraft, there are various ways to interact with it. 

* **Direct Control** - You can directly control the probe using the same controls as your spacecraft. 
* **AI Control** - You can use the AI co-pilot to control the probe. 
* **Autonomous Control** - You can set the probe to operate autonomously, collecting data and broadcasting signals on its own. 
* **Relay** - Probes can be used to relay signals from other probes or from your spacecraft. 
* **Sensors** - Probes can be equipped with a variety of sensors to collect data about the environment, which can be transmitted back to the spacecraft or broadcasted as signals. 
* **Broadcast** - Probes can be used to broadcast signals, such as distress signals, research data, or other information. 
* **Resource Collection** - Probes that can return to the spacecraft can be called back to the spacecraft to deliver resources.

## Probe Limitations
* **Power** - Probes have limited power.
* **Range** - Probes have limited range.
* **Durability** - Probes are fragile, and can be destroyed by asteroids, radiation, or other hazards.
* **Size** - Probes are small, and can only carry a limited amount of resources.

## Signal Dynamics
Communication with probes is subject to the same signal dynamics as other signals in the game. This means that signals can be blocked by planets, moons, asteroids, and other objects. Signals can also be weakened by distance, and can be distorted by atmospheric conditions.
