# Spacecraft
This document describes the spacecraft in the game There Exists. 

## Spacecraft Systems
Every spacecraft has a unique set of systems. Your spacecraft will start with a random set of systems, and you can upgrade or replace them over time. 

### Flight Systems
Flight systems are used to control the movement of the spacecraft. A spacecraft usually contains smaller thrusters for fine adjustments and larger thrusters for major maneuvers. The fundamental measure of a ship's capability is its delta-v (Δv) budget — the total change in velocity it can produce before exhausting its propellant. Every burn draws from this finite budget, and managing it is the central challenge of navigation. It is important to distinguish between propellant (the mass ejected to generate thrust) and power (what drives the engine) — some engines consume large quantities of propellant but need no external power, while others are very propellant-efficient but require a significant power source. Below are some examples of flight systems:

* Chemical Thrusters
* Ion Thrusters
* Hall Effect Thrusters
* Solar Sails
* Plasma Thrusters

### Sensor Systems
The various sensor systems are used to detect objects and phenomena in space. This is detailed in the Sensors document.

### Probe Systems
Probes are unmanned vehicles that can be sent to explore distant star systems. They are equipped with a variety of sensors and can be used to collect data about the environment. Probes can be launched from a ship or from a planet. This is detailed in the Probes document.

### Propellant Storage
Propellant storage is separate from the ship's power supply. The type and quantity of propellant a ship carries directly determines its Δv budget — a full tank and an empty one represent entirely different ships in terms of capability. Propellant can be resupplied from ice deposits, gas giant skimming, or asteroid mining, making resource gathering a direct extension of flight range.

### Structural Systems
The hull and internal structure of a spacecraft must withstand micrometeorite impacts, radiation, and the stress of thermal cycling between extreme heat and cold. Ships require radiation shielding to protect crew and electronics, though heavier shielding increases dry mass and reduces Δv. Hull integrity must be tracked and maintained — a compromised hull risks depressurization, and repeated thermal stress degrades components over time.

### Engineering Systems
Engineering systems are used to repair, build, and upgrade the spacecraft. Depending on the plans your Spacecraft OS has access to, you can build and upgrade your ship. Building requires resources, time, and power. 

### Life Support Systems
Life support systems are used to keep the crew alive. Depending on what planet you came from, this can mean different things. Different levels of pressure, temperature, humidity, and atmospheric composition may be required to keep you alive. The ships sensors and life support systems can be used to determine what is required to keep you alive. 

### Thermal Management Systems
In a vacuum, heat can only be shed through thermal radiation — there is no convection. Every active system generates waste heat, and if it accumulates faster than it can be radiated away, the ship will begin to fail. Ships use large radiator panels to shed heat continuously, and heat sinks to absorb short bursts of excess heat that the radiators cannot handle immediately. Pilots must manage their thermal headroom — the margin between their current heat load and the ship's limit — especially when running multiple systems at once. A ship's heat signature is also detectable by sensors at great distances, making thermal output a factor in how visible the ship is.

### Power Systems
The spacecraft can be powered by a variety of sources. Some are renewable, while others are not. Some are more powerful than others. Some are more reliable than others. Below are some examples of power sources:

* Solar Panels
* Nuclear Fission Reactors
* Nuclear Fusion Reactors
* Antimatter Reactors
* Fuel Cells

The spacecraft uses power to run various systems and can store power in batteries. Additionally, pilots can manage the distribution of power between systems to optimize performance or conserve power.
