# Sensors
This document describes the sensors that can be installed on a spacecraft in the game There Exists.

## Sensor Categories
There are three categories of sensors: Active, Passive, and Internal.

### Active Sensors
Active sensors emit a signal and measure the response. They are useful for detecting objects at a distance, but they can also be detected by other ships. Below are some examples of active sensors.

* Radar
* Lidar
* Neutron Spectrometer

### Passive Sensors
Passive sensors detect signals that are emitted by other objects. They are useful for detecting objects at a distance, but they can also be detected by other ships. Below are some examples of passive sensors.

* Radio Telescopes
* Photometer
* Cosmic Ray Detector

### Internal Sensors
Internal sensors are sensors that are installed on the spacecraft itself. They are useful for monitoring the health and status of the spacecraft. Below are some examples of internal sensors.

* Strain Gauges
* Mass Spectrometer
* Fuel Gauge
* Inertial Measurement Unit

## Sensor Usage
### Power
Sensors require power to operate. The amount of power required depends on the type of sensor and the type of environment. Sometimes the user can turn on and off sensors to conserve power, but this can also limit their ability to detect objects.

### Environment
Sensors can be affected by the environment. For example, some sensors may not work in a vacuum, while others may be affected by radiation.

### Detection
Sensors can be used to detect objects at a distance. The range of detection depends on the type of sensor, the amount of power it is receiving, and the type of environment. Pilots can configure sensors to notify them when they detect objects of interest.
